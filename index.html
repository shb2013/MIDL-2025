Segmentation‑Informed Captioning: A Multi‑Stage Pipeline for Surgical Vision–Language Dataset Generation

Abstract

Surgical vision‑language models require high‑quality paired image‑text data, yet current datasets derived from audio transcripts are noisy and poorly aligned. We introduce a five‑stage pipeline that converts existing segmentation data into clean, spatially & temporally grounded captions by progressively adding object lists, absolute and relative positions, proximity‑based interaction inference, and clip‑level temporal summaries. Expert evaluation of 600 captions showed that 95 % were rated ≥3/5, with GPT‑4o consistently achieving the highest quality. Our method provides a reliable path to create large‑scale, clinically coherent data for training robust surgical VLMs, as detailed in the poster below.
